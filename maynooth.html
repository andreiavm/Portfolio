Maynooth Furniture

my role: design and development

project type: personal

(work in progress)

A personal project I have worked on while teaching myself design and development. It is a fictive furniture store built with a nordic feel, and the target audience would be middle class people who are interested in purchasing custom quality furniture pieces.

view website

moodboard:

Before I started designing this project, I did a quick desk research on other stores to see what their traffic is and whether their traffic comes from mobile devices or desktop devices. After finding out, surprisingly, that the traffic is split up in almost equal parts, 60 percent for the web, and 40 percent for mobile, I decided I need to focus on both the mobile and desktop versions of the interface.

After researching the market, I started generating ideas for this project. In order to be able to visualise my ideas better, I created a moodboard with similar stores that I found inspirational.

The moodboard is one of my go-to methods of ideation, and it helps me to bring my ideas to life. I search components and colours that are similar to the one I have in mind and I get a better understanding of how my future design should look like. The moodboard supports me through the whole design process, and in case I feel stuck, I can come back to it and find my inspiration.

Finally, I designed the high-fidelity prototype, and after that I developed the website using html and css.

styleguide:

After creating the moodboard, I also created a style-guide for the design. I went with some really soft colours that speak simplicity, elegance and luxury. For the font choices, I went with a combination that you can never go wrong with: two minimalistic, easy to read sans serifs.

mindmap:

After building the moodboard and the wireframes, I worked on imagining the information architecture of the website, and I visualised this data by using a mindmap that illustrated every hypothetical flow the user could take while navigating through the website.
