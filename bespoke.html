Bespoke Design

my role: research, ideation, design and prototyping

project type: university assignment

Conceptual project for university, in which we designed an application that should fulfill the needs of one user. The scope of this project was to learn how to empathize and build an experience that is crafted according to the userâ€™s needs, context, personality and lifestyle.

view prototype

the problem:

For this project we had to translate the 'old' rituals of giving and receiving gifts to the digital context, so the giving of intangible gifts becomes as meaningful as getting regular tangible gifts. The gift giving experience consists of four elements that work together to tell a consistent and coherent story that helps strengthen the memory of the gift: announcement, unwrapping, reveal and resolution.

the research:

In order to craft an experience that is bespoke for our user, first we had to perform user research. We converged and ideated our research questions, and then we diverged and started performing different methods of ethnographic research.

affinity diagram:

Sequently to performing four different methods of ethnographic research and collecting all the data, we converged again and created an affinity diagram that groups all the data into clusters, from which I extracted the user insights. The image is blurred because I didn't get the permision from my user to use his private information in the public domain.

user persona:

I used all the previously collected data to build an user portrait. For this case study, because of privacy reasons, I chose to convert the portrait into a fictional persona with the same characteristics, and replace his private pictures with stock images.

ideation:

After emphatizing with the user and defining the context and the problem, the next step in the design thinking process was to generate solutions.

the methods:

We created HMW questions related to each step of the gift giving experience, and then we diverged again and used different methods of ideation in two different group sessions. Here I acted as a facilitator and helped our team ideate solutions by using different creative methods.

low fidelity prototype:

After converging all of our generated ideas, we identified clear solutions to the problem. The next step in the design thinking process was to diverge again and build prototypes of the concept we previously generated.

We built a paper prototype and tested the core features of our application. We used this "cheap" method of prototyping in order to conserve resources and make mistakes that are easy to correct, during the incipient stage of our prototyping.

We converged again and performed two rounds of testing on two different iterations of the lo-fi prototypes, where we identified usability problems with our designed experience.

high fidelity prototype:

Using the feedback from the testing of the low fidelity prototypes, we diverged again and built two different versions of the high fidelity prototype, and tested both of them with different users.

user testing:

As a final step in the design thinking process, we tested our high fidelity prototypes with multiple users, but also with Marcus for whom we were building this experience.

The results were surprising, and after testing the hi-fi prototype we decided to tweak a few of the aesthetic choices, because the user felt like these don't really work for him. More importantly, we changed the flow of the application for the reveal moment, because the testing proved that it wasn't as surprising as we first thought.

By changing the flow and a few of the main components of the application, we improved both the usability and the interactivity, thus making it more engaging and stimulating for our user.

final results:

The result of this 8 weeks long project is a quiz game with memory boxes, designed with a modern, brutalistic aesthetic that fits the end-user.

For the annoucement, the user recieves a QR code, and by scanning it, he accesses the unwrapping experience. The user opens all the boxes in sequencial order, and with each box he recieves a question that is related to his personality and interests.

As the unwrapping takes place, each question reveals the surprise little by little, and in the end the reveal moment happens and the user gets his intangible gift, which was a plane ticket to Italy.

After the reveal moment, the resolution gives the user the option to come back to the app and upload a picture from the holiday, thus wrapping up the whole experience nicely with an end goal.

what I've learned:

While working on this project, I have learned how to apply the design thinking process and how to iterate through the different stages of it, by converging and diverging.

I got a grasp on what it takes to work in a fast paced environment, as this whole project was executed in weekly sprints, and I understood the importance of research for design.

Finally, I understood how important soft skills are when working in a multicultural team of designers, and how to adapt to the diffrent styles of communication each person on the team has.
